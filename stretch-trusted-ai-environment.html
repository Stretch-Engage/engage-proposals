The question every leader is asking

"Can we use AI on our data — safely?"

The instinct is right and the hesitation is right. AI agents are genuinely useful against CRM data: surfacing account health, summarising open work, drafting follow-ups. But the common ways of doing it are uncomfortable for good reason — pasting records into a public chat tool, handing a third-party service your org credentials, or wiring Salesforce straight to an open AI endpoint.

We built a different setup. The AI works against your data through a controlled gateway that runs inside your own network. Your credentials never leave your perimeter, the AI only ever sees what you decided to expose, and every action it takes is recorded. You get to experiment with what AI can do — on a foundation your security team can actually stand behind.

What makes it trusted

Four properties you can demonstrate, not just claim

01

Your data never leaves your perimeter

The gateway and your Salesforce credentials run on infrastructure you control. There are no inbound ports open to the internet, and no org credentials are handed to any third party. The connection is outbound-only and encrypted end to end.

02

The AI only sees what you allow

Every request passes through a fixed set of approved capabilities. The AI cannot run open-ended queries against your org — its reach is exactly the list of functions we agree on, each scoped to a specific purpose. That list is finite and reviewable.

03

Reading is open, changing is gated

The AI can summarise and surface information freely. Anything that changes a record requires explicit human confirmation in the moment — the AI cannot modify, delete, or send on its own initiative.

04

Every action is logged

Each thing the AI does is recorded — what was accessed, when, and on whose behalf. If a question is ever raised, there is a complete and reviewable trail rather than a black box.

What we will not claim

No one has "solved" AI safety — and we won't pretend to

Our position

The risk that an AI agent can be influenced by the very content it reads is a known, industry-wide property of how today's AI works — true of every model and every vendor. Anyone who tells you they have eliminated it is overstating.

What we do instead is engineer the environment so that the consequences are contained: the AI's reach is small, its actions are reversible and recorded, and anything that matters requires a person. We don't claim the risk is gone. We make sure that if something is attempted, it can't do damage and we can see exactly what happened.

In our experience this is the answer a serious security review wants to hear. It reflects how the threat actually works, and it puts the discipline where it belongs — in the architecture around the AI, not in marketing promises about the AI itself.

How we'd start with you
